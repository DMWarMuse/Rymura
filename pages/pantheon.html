Religion, Magic, and the Pantheon

The information below is incomplete, there are additional gods that are not named below (some are listed). If you do not see a god or patron that will fit your character or playstyle, please let me know and I will work with you to create one.

The Warrens

Clerical and Magical energies permeate the world through founts of the forces, powers, and components of the Prime Material Plane. These forces were there during the infancy of the world; pure, and present in all things.

Each source dwells inside a Warren (sometimes called a House or Hold). These Warrens are sub-plane repositories; pure representations of the power that resides within. Venture into the Warren of Order and you will be treated to straight lines, uniformity, and predictability, where-as the Warren of Purity is a vast un-marked snow-covered landscape of peace and contentment.

Each Warren is overseen by an effigy the reflects the power that that Warren is composed of. The Warren of Fate, for example, is presided over by The Inexorable; A being of unstoppable inevitability. The Warren of Destruction, on the other hand, is presided over by The Architect of Ruin; worried about nothing else other than the complete dismantling of all things. These effigies are kept in check by the others in a delicate dance of balance, and they rarely turn their attention outside of their own dominion.

The raw power of a Warren is too much for standard mortals to harness and control; while incredible feats can be performed, in the end the body and mind will shatter. Paragons, Exemplars, and other Higher beings, known colloquially as Gods, temper the flow of the Warrens and disseminate its power to its users.

"The Warrens of the world dwell beyond the planes. Find the gate and nudge it open a crack. What leaks out is yours to shape. Open yourself to the Warren that comes to you - that finds you. Draw forth its power - as much as your body and soul are capable of containing - but remember, when the body fails, the gate closes."― Tattersail

Description of Gods

The Following are short descriptions of the Gods of Rymura (and extraplanar gods): Listed Alphabetically.
More detailed descriptions and write-ups of gods are available if interested. Example Here

Agrinor

God of Suffering, Plague and Pestilence

Maxim: The corrupter of flesh.
Symbol: A Locust Leg
Common Followers:  Poisoners, Torturers, Malcontents: Not worshiped in the open.
Description: Besides being invoked as a curse against someone whom you don’t like, Agrinor is not talked about in polite conversation.  The superstition is that voicing his name will bring ruin and despair down upon you.  He delights in designing new plagues and sending them out into the world.

Amys

Goddess of Divinations

Maxim: The Pulled Thread.
Symbol: A Crystal Orb
Common Followers:  Scholars, Bone Casters, Fortune Tellers.
Description: Believed to have been lost, or at least extremely weakened, in the Frist Battle of Heavens Fall; the use of tarot cards and bone casters hinted that Amys might have retained her power and gone into hiding.  This was proven accurate after she appeared to, and helped, the Stone Bearers in their fight against Tarmeer in the events preceding The Second Battle of Heavens Fall some 1600 years later.

Astalon

Deceased God of Law, Order, and Justice

Maxim: The Peacemaker.
Symbol: A Gold Key
Common Followers: Barristers, kings, nobility, headsman, constables.
Description:  The divine representative of peace, order, and law, Astalon was devoutly worshipped by nobility, magistrates, and constables.  He was slain by The Unbound One during The Second Battle of Heavens Fall.

Banath

God of Thieves and Masks

Maxim: The Weighted Dice.
Symbol: Inverted Triangle with Circled Point
Common Followers:  Thieves, Con-men, Spy’s.
Description:Those who primarily deal in deception, thievery, and lies worship Banath.  You will likely never see his holy symbol displayed out in the open, unless you know where to look.  Tithes are sometimes offered at the church of Banath in an effort to ward off thieves and protect cargo.

Byron

God of Artisans and Inspiration

Maxim: The great entertainer.  The tireless worker.
Symbol: Harp or Smiths Hammer.
Common Followers: Entertainers such as writers, poets, artists, and actors, as well as artisans such as carpenters, stonemasons, wheelwrights, and blacksmiths.
Description: The followers of Byron are staunchly devoted.  Worshipers believe their art or craft lives and dies at his will.  Dwarves and blacksmiths see Byron as the forge master, they invoke his name when trying to make items of special significance or certain prominence.

Dicium

God of Law and Obedience

Maxim: The Iron Hand.
Symbol: Three Vertical Bars in Parallel
Common Followers: Kings, Nobility, Executioners, Constables, Barristers.
Description: Eldest sister of Somniatella and Vora.  Originally a Goddess of Death and Judgement during the Time of Turmoil, Dicium released the sole moniker of death to Kulindra and took up the mantle of law after Astalon was slain by The Unbound One in The Second Battle of Heavens Fall.

Dubh Catha

Lord of the Shadowfell

Maxim: Lord of Black, The Dark Crow
Symbol: Crow head in profile
Common Followers: Darklings, some Firbolgs, other denizens of the Shadowfell.
Description: Cast out of Queen Tatiana’s court in the Feywild.  Dubh Catha quickly became feared in the Shadowfells.  Together with his Darkling brethren, he builds his power for his inevitable strike against the Feywild and Tatiana.  He is at odds with The Raven Queen as she too wishes to control the Shadowfell.

Agents:

Jaggerjack:  Located in the Prime Material Plane, Jaggerjack belongs to a sect of Firbolgs who are far more militaristic and violent then their nature inclined cousins.  He was one of the Stone Bearers and escaped The Second Battle of Heavens Fall with the help of Dubh Catha.  Jaggerjack is now a Darkling highbred who leads an army of Firbolgs and Darklings serving Dubh Catha’s will. 

Dulmarth

God of Protection, Courage, and Self-Sacrifice

Maxim: Shield of the Weak.
Symbol: White Heater Shield.
Common Followers: Downtrodden, urchins, orphanages, some soldiers.
Description: Brother of Kelmarik, Dulmarth is prayed to for protection from harm by those who have seen the worst in the world.  He is also turned to by soldiers who plan on sacrificing themselves for the good of others; they pray for a noble and heroic end.  Dulmarth is often seen with El Morin.

El-Morin

Goddess of Winter, Solitude, and Loss

Maxim: The Sound of Silence.
Symbol: Blue Snowflake
Common Followers: Outcasts, wanderers, hermits, and those that enjoy solitude.
Description: Sister to Kirallin, the goddess of winter is typically worshipped by hermits and those that cherish the solitude and silence of night.  Many say a prayer to El-Morin during the Midwinter festival.  Seen as neither benevolent nor malicious.

Agents:

Saint Silindus – The Rear Guard:  Saint Silindus was one of the adventurers that catalyzed the Second Battle of Heavens Fall.  After falling in battle at the Temple of the Three Sisters, El Morin and Dulmarth recruited him to their service in exchange for allowing The Order of the White River to reside in the Warren of Purity.  Silindus now serves Dulmarth as he sends him out into the Prime Material Plane to assist those who are worthy.

Einhasad

God of The Hunt.

Maxim: The Red Jawed Hunter.
Symbol: Arrow Knocked Bow
Common Followers: Hunters, scouts, rangers, and those affected with Lycanthropy.
Description: Einhasad has been represented as both a man and a wolf.  Hunters ask for his blessing when taking a killing shot, and scouts say a prayer that they will remain hidden.  Because of old scripture describing Einhasad as a wolf, he is also worshiped by those afflicted with lycanthropy.

Eva

Goddess of Farming and Agriculture.

Maxim: Lady Spring.
Symbol: A Sheaf of Wheat
Common Followers: Farmers, apothecarists, and herbalists.
Description:  Eva is seen as a benevolent god who likes to see her followers safe and comfortable.  When a farmer has a better than average crop, they praise Eva, but when hail or locusts ruin them, they blame Tarmeer or Agrinor.

Familor

God of Servitude and Chains

Maxim: The pact maker.
Symbol: Black Manacles
Common Followers: Some Warlocks
Description: Familor likes to strike bargains.  He will offer salvation, dangling it in front of his victim in exchange for a favor, until that favor is complete Familor keeps the bargainers’ soul.  Those who have successfully fulfilled one of his pacts tell of horrific dreams for months and years after; it is rumored that Familor likes to torture his souls while they are in his possession.  There has never been a (known) case of Familor breaking his word and keeping a soul beyond the completed favor.  Some Warlocks look to Familor for power, swearing fealty to him in exchange servitude.

Great Grandmother Stemrot

Powerful Night Hag

Maxim:
Symbol: A Flayed Rat
Common Followers: Some Hags and Warlocks
Description: Little is known about Great Grandmother Stemrot, other than her name and that other Hags fear her.  Her location is unknown, but her symbol was found on some crates in a cargo ship being used by the Stone Bearers.  The crates contained black puddings and there were Faceless aboard.  It is thought that the attack was a message of displeasure after the adventurers killed a different Hag: Aunty Agatha.

Guis-wa

Deceased God of Endurance, Prowess, and Physical Skill.

Maxim: The Tireless Runner.
Symbol: An Arm - Shown Flexed.
Common Followers: Soldiers, duelists, festival attendees.
Description: The god of endurance and skill was primarily worshiped at festivals and sporting events.  Seen as a benevolent god, it is hoped he will show you favor and allow you to win the competition.  Soldiers and duelists were also known to worship Guis-wa, even going so far as to refer the network of trenches, wooden barricades, and earthen berms between Parnathen and Tarmentis as Guis-wa’s Wall. He was slain in the Temple of the Three Sisters during the Second Battle of Heavens Fall by Kelmarik, with help from her brother Dulmarth.

Ishtar

Goddess of Fortune and Luck

Maxim: The nudge.
Symbol: Two white dice - one pip and six pips
Common Followers: Gamblers, those undertaking risky endeavors, those in a tight spot, or those going after fortune.  Some thieves and sailors also worship Ishtar.
Description:  The Lady Ishtar had a larger following amongst the denizens of underworld until losing much of her power during the First Battle of Heaves Fall; this allowed Banath to usurp much of Ishtar’s domain.  Ishtar does not discriminate.  All humanoids want something, and all will risk things to get it, she is merely there to see, or help, the dice fall.   Her name will often be used as a curse when befallen by a bad decision, or ill luck.

Kelmarik

Goddess of Vengeance and Revenge.

Maxim: The Slow Burn.
Symbol: An Inverted Flame.
Common Followers: Bounty hunters, some assassins, and those done wrong.
Description: Sister to Dulmarth, Kelmarik is invoked by those seeking vengeance, payback, or revenge.  Rarely steadily worshiped (except by bounty hunters), she is turned to in times of need with great zeal.  Her name is often spoken as a curse.

Kirallin

Goddess of Family, Society, and Home

Maxim: The Open Hearth.
Symbol: A Lily
Common Followers: Expecting mothers, Elders, many villagers.
Description: Sister of El-Morin, Kirallin is a benevolent goddess worshipped by many who find family and home most important in life.  She is often portrayed as a kind elderly grandmother.

Kulindra (Kul)

Goddess of Death

Maxim: She who waits.  The caster of nets.
Symbol: A Silver Net
Common Followers: Priests, Grave Keepers, some Assassins and Healers.
Description: When a mortal dies its soul is ushered to Kulindra’s Gate, there it sits in judgement from Kulindra herself for how the soul lived in life.  Priests of Kulindra will seek out, and attempt to destroy, necromancy at any costs as it goes against the natural order of the universe.  The priests of Kulindra do not seem to age, you may see an old priestess, but she will look in her early twenties.  Most priests and priestesses of the goddess of death have a morose aura about them, and lifeless eyes.  Kulindra is at odds with The Raven Queen as she is the Goddess of Undeath.

Loial

­God of Travel and Adventure

Maxim: The Tireless Wanderer.
Symbol: A Worn Boot or A Wagon Wheel.
Common Followers: Merchants, peddlers, travelers, adventurers, and wanderers.
Description:  Loial is also known as the god of the open road.  Worshiped by merchants and wanderers alike, many will say a quick prayer to Loial before starting a long journey.

Mar

­God of the Sea

Maxim: The Ruler of Waves
Symbol: Mar's Fishhook
Common Followers: Sailors, Fishermen, Shipwrights.
Description: Back when they were mortals, Mar was Tarpalm's captain on the Dreadnought's Regard. Tarpalm led a mutiny on the ship and threw Mar overboard into the deep. He waited there, biding his time, while Tarpalm usurped his domain. A chance drowning by another sailor many years later allowed him an escape and a chance to regain his domain.

Agents:

Jeremy Gifford: Another victim of a sea voyage gone awry, he managed to sink, by chance, in the same vicinity of Mar. A shell of his former self, Mar recruited Giff into his service - granting him powers in exchange for helping return the god to his former glory.

Neltelantol

­The Great Dryad

Maxim: The Mother of Trees
Symbol: A Gold Leaf
Common Followers: Most Elves in the Feywild, and the Majority of Elves in the Prime Material Plane.
Description:  Neltelantol dwells deep in the Feywild.  She is the source of most of the Elven Spellweavers powers, making the majority of Tel’Na’Gren and Tarcian spell casters warlocks.  She watches over her subjects in the Fey and in the Prime Material Plane with endearment and love as they share the same affinity for growing and green things as she does.

Relomdra, Demon Lord

Goddess of Havoc

Maxim: The Sower of Seeds
Symbol: Blue Sword on a Blood Drop
Common Followers: Corrupted Cultists and Demon Worshipers
Description: Former Goddess of War and Strife.  Relomdra was cast into the Abyss by The Unbound One during the Second Battle of Heavens Fall, she quickly returned to the battlefield as a Demon Lord leading an army from The Abyss.  She was struck down and was forced to retreat into the Abyss by (then mortal) Saint Thule and Saint Silindas at the Temple of the Three Sisters.

Sa-Sarig

God of Illusion.

Maxim: The Unseen.
Symbol: A Piece of Reflective Metal.
Common Followers: Illusionists and Some Magic Users.
Description:  Sa-Sarig gained power and influence after The First Battle of Heaves Fall when he took over the illusion domain from Sarzerik who was destroyed.  Those who worship Sa-Sarig often embody his mischievous nature.

Somniatella

Goddess of Dreams and of the Night

Maxim: She who walks in shadow.
Symbol: Four Pointed Star
Common Followers: Mages, Orphans, some Vampires
Description:  Middle Sister to Dicium and Vora.  Somniatella has been known to visit users dreams and give them advice or assistance; the sleeper will often wake up with an item they do not remember owning.  The most gifted of Somniatellas followers are known as Dream Walkers, able to physically enter peoples dreams and communicate with them, or use it as a method of travel by exiting a user’s dream within their physical vicinity.  She is known as Goddess of the Night, as such, some Vampires have taken up worship of her.

Sylinus

­The Great Satyr

Maxim: The Horn of Battle
Symbol: Satyr Horns
Common Followers: Most Elves in the Feywild, and the Majority of Elves in the Prime Material Plane.
Description:  While all elves acknowledge Neltelentol’s power and domain over nature, many elves also pay respect to Sylinus, the great satyr protector who guards the Fey from enemies and embodies the very essence of a warrior: vigilance, dedication, and passion.  Elven warriors who have proven themselves in battle are often seen with satyr horns embroidered on their armor.

Tarmeer

Deceased God of Storms and of the Sea

Maxim: The Sudden Squall, The Fickle Mistress.
Symbol: A Lightning Bolt or Anchor.
Common Followers: Sailors, island dwellers, and fishermen.
Description: During The First Battle of Heaves Fall Tarpalm, god of storms, and Numeer, goddess of the sea, were defeated.  Already lovers, the two chose to combine their powers and meld into one entity lest they be permanently destroyed, cast out, and forgotten.  Tarmeer was seen as neither good nor evil yet was staunchly worshiped by sailors and fishermen.  Tarmeer’s name was often used as a curse, or a boon, depending on the situation.  Tarmeer fell at the hands of The Stone Bearers, with the help of Amys, at the Temple of the Three Sisters.

Tiana

Goddess of Magic

Maxim: The Lady of Magic.
Symbol: A Sunburst
Common Followers: Mages, Sorcerers, Shamans.
Description: The original Goddess of Magic dating back before The First Battle of Heaves Fall, she went into hiding and was working on restoring her power under the guise of Enchantress Tiana, Mage of the Rillnon Court.  It was Tiana who instructed Amys to wait and assist the Stone Bearers when they arrived in the Temple of the Three Sisters.  At the conclusion of The Second Battle of Heavens Fall, Tiana regained her full power.

Agents:

Giannis Fairburn: Giannis was one of the adventurers that catalyzed the Second Battle of Heavens Fall.  Previous to those events, she gifted a ring to Giannis that saved his life.  He now serves her for the greater good.  Giannis is the original creator of the spell: Fairburn’s Parley.

Tonks

God of Knowledge and Wisdom

Maxim: The Chronicler.
Symbol: A Codex Scroll
Common Followers: Scholars, Historians, Librarians.
Description: Tonks was one of the gods released from the stones in The Second Battle of Heavens Fall.  He is a neutral god that parts his wisdom on those he sees fit.  Students are known to say a prayer to Tonks before an important exam.  There is mention of Tonks in the Rescue of the Belevar Kingdom, a children’s story about the hero Talon, who enters the Warren Arcana and seeks out Tonks to find the answer to an illness that has befallen his queen.  This has never been corroborated as anything other than fantasy.

The Unbound One

God of Madness, Ruler of Chaos Hold.

Maxim: The One Who Sees.
Symbol: unknown
Common Followers:
Description: The Unbound One is a being of pure madness.  As such, he is not bound by the rules of the Warrens and often walks the Prime Material Plane causing havoc and devastation to further his own ends.  He was once defeated and locked away in the Prime Material Plane by an alliance of the other Warrens, including The Abstainer.  He was mistakenly set free by the Stone Bearers.  He was last seen at the Battle at Three Sisters resembling a frail old man wielding an oversized scythe.  He is responsible for the death of Astalon, and the turning of Relomdra into a Demon Lord.

Vora

Goddess of Battle and War

Maxim: The Lady of Swords.
Symbol: Two Thin Swords in Parallel
Common Followers: Soldiers and Mercenaries.
Description: Youngest sister of Dicium and Somniatella.  Vora was the one who directed the elf Merrowquin and set into motion the events preceding The Second Battle of Heavens Fall.  It was in the Temple of the Three Sisters where she was released from the stones and fought with Relomdra.  Relomdra was defeated and Vora became the only effigy of War.  Her followers now hunt down the remaining followers of Demon Lord Relomdra who have become tainted by the Abyss.

Agents:

Thule:  Half-orc Barbarian and the only (known) living Saint.  Thule, one of the Stone Bearers, is the Harbinger of Battle and serves at Vora’s whim.  He, and his wife and daughter, chief the Hasation Clan of orcs.  His last known whereabouts were in northern Rymura, hunting down Demon Lord Relomdra’s cultists.

Vrandur

God of Fire

Maxim: The Burner of Cities, The Bringer of Light
Symbol: A Torch
Common Followers: Fanatics and the Fool-hearty.
Description: Vrandur the NG God of Light, Fire, and Warmth was almost destroyed during The First Battle of Heavens Fall.  The last Priests of Vrandur deposited a living fragment of the god into the child Chulainn.  Hoping that the deity would stay hidden from his hunters until his power could be restored.  Unfortunately, the life of Chulainn was sad and tragic, the chaotic side of Vrandur ended up consuming Chulainn during The Second Battle of Heavens Fall.  Now, most of those that worship him wish to see destruction.  A small number of worshipers attempt to coerce Vrandur back to benevolence.
