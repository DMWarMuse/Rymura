Description of Gods and Saints

The Following are short descriptions of the Gods of Rymura (and extraplanar gods): Listed Alphabetically.
More detailed descriptions and write-ups of gods are available if interested. Example Here

Agrinor the Unclean

Saint of Suffering and Pestilence

Maxim: The corrupter of flesh
Symbol: A Locust Leg
Description: Besides being invoked as a curse against someone whom you don’t like, Agrinor is not talked about in polite conversation. The superstition is that voicing his name will bring ruin and despair down upon you and your loved ones. Agrinors followers believe that when a land gets too corrupt, its people to pompous, or stray too far from the light of the gods, he will remind them of their place with a plague or devastation.

The Ordained: Agrinor's ordained, few that there are, call themselves Plague Bearers. They believe each disease, sickness, or devastation is a gift and a chance for the inflicted to reflect on how good their lives were, benefiting from the added perspective of an imminent death. The Plague Bearers design new plagues and send them out into the world: reminding the races that they are mortal and not above the gods.

Known Sects/Cults: Black Alchemists - As the name would suggest, the members of this sect are underworld Alchemists and Leeches. They usually use their knowledge to help the sick and dying, for a price, believing that Agrinor only intends his subjects to suffer, not die.

Amys the Sibyl

Saint of Divinations

Maxim: The Pulled Thread
Symbol: A Crystal Orb
Description: Amys is believed to send visions, glimpses of the possible futures, to individuals whos inaction could cause untold devastation. When Deja Vu is witnessed it is believed that Amys is paying direct attention to whatever events are unfolding.

Believed to have been lost, or at least extremely weakened, in the First Battle of Heavens Fall; the use of tarot cards and bone casters hinted that Amys might have retained her power and gone into hiding. This was proven accurate after she appeared to, and helped, the Stone Bearers in their fight against Tarmeer in the events preceding The Second Battle of Heavens Fall some years later.

The Ordained: Oracles of Amys are known to possess potent foresight abilities. They are often called upon, or appear, at the crossroads of great decisions that shape future events. They believe that Amys provides information of what could happen, not what will happen, and that it is up to the individual to use that information, not for the Oracles to intervene.

Banath the Master of Masks

Saint of Thievery

Maxim: The Weighted Dice.
Symbol: Inverted Triangle with Circled Point
Tenets: Thieves Proser, The Rich must Remember
Description: You will likely never see his holy symbol displayed out in the open unless you know where to look. Tithes are sometimes offered at the church of Banath in an effort to ward off thieves and protect cargo.

The Ordained: The ordained of Banath (Called Masks) live by the two tenets: "Thieves Prosper" and "The Rich must Remember". They do their best to help, and hide, members of the various brotherhoods of thieves, and to remind the privileged of the world that they are not gods; dealing out what could be their only punishment this side of divine retribution.

Byron the Forgemaster

Saint of Artisans and Culture

Maxim: The Blacksmith Poet. The Tireless Worker
Symbol: Harp or Smiths Hammer.
Description: Also known as Byron the Poet, followers of Byron are staunchly devoted. Worshipers believe their art or craft lives and dies at his will. Blacksmiths invoke his name when trying to make items of special significance or certain prominence.

The Ordained: False-Facers

Dicium the Law Giver

God of Law and Order

Maxim: The Iron Hand.
Symbol: Three Vertical Bars in Parallel
Description: Eldest sister of Somniatella and Vora. Originally a Goddess of Death and Judgement during the Time of Turmoil, Dicium released the sole moniker of death to Kulindra and took up the mantle of law after Astalon was slain by The Unbound One in The Second Battle of Heavens Fall.

The Ordained: Adjudicators of Dicium routinely travel to outlying villages and hamlets and help settle disputes or act as judges. Adjudicators can also be requested during court proceedings as they are known to be impartial in their judgement.

Dubh Catha

Lord of the Shadowfell

Maxim: Lord of Black, The Dark Crow
Symbol: Crow head in profile
Common Followers: Darklings, some Firbolgs, other denizens of the Shadowfell.
Description: Cast out of Queen Tatiana’s court in the Feywild. Dubh Catha quickly became feared in the Shadowfells. Together with his Darkling brethren, he builds his power for his inevitable strike against the Feywild and Tatiana. He is at odds with The Raven Queen as she too wishes to control the Shadowfell.
More Info

Agents:

Jaggerjack: Located in the Prime Material Plane, Jaggerjack belongs to a sect of Firbolgs who are far more militaristic and violent then their nature inclined cousins. He was one of the Stone Bearers and escaped The Second Battle of Heavens Fall with the help of Dubh Catha. Jaggerjack is now a Darkling highbred who leads an army of Firbolgs and Darklings serving Dubh Catha’s will.

Dulmarth the Cleansed

Saint of Redemption

Maxim: Shield of the Weak.
Symbol: White Heater Shield.
Description: Brother of Kelmarik. Most temples dedicated to Dulmarth also double as orphanages. He is also prayed to by soldiers who plan on sacrificing themselves for the good of others; they pray for a noble and heroic end.

The Ordained: Members of the Vigilance of Dulmarth travel from village to village on the outskirts of civilization lending their protection to anyone who has need. Often they can be seen traveling with merchant caravans who only need pay them in food.

Known Sects/Cults: The Pale Hand - An austere group dedicated to collecting donations and coin for the less fortunate.

El-Morin the Silent

Saint of Winter, Solitude, and Peace

Maxim: The Lady of Long Silence.
Symbol: Blue Snowflake
Description: Sister to Kirallin, the Saint of Winter is typically worshipped by hermits and those that cherish the solitude and silence of night. Many say a prayer to El-Morin during the Midwinter festival. Seen as neither benevolent nor malicious.

Agents:

Saint Silindus – The Rear Guard: Saint Silindus was one of the adventurers that catalyzed the Second Battle of Heavens Fall. After falling in battle at the Temple of the Three Sisters, El Morin and Dulmarth recruited him to their service in exchange for allowing The Order of the White River to reside in the Warren of Purity. Silindus now serves Dulmarth as he sends him out into the Prime Material Plane to assist those who are worthy.

Einhasad the Preserver

Saint of Untamed Nature.

Maxim: The Red Jawed Hunter.
Symbol: White Tread-Softly Flower on a Green Circle
Description: Einhasad has been represented as both a man and a wolf. Hunters ask for his blessing when taking a killing shot, and scouts say a prayer that they will remain hidden. Because of old scripture describing Einhasad as a wolf, he is also worshiped by those afflicted with lycanthropy.

The Ordained: The Hunters of Einhasad are skilled members of the church that everyday citizens can petition to take care of the random and roving beast. A Swamp Troll is threating a small hamlet? A pack of Griffons are killing livestock? Call in a Hunter.

Known Sects/Cults: The Divine Teeth - A group of werewolves who seek out and destroy evil; they see themselves as the divine hand of Einhasad. They will also destroy those who have been affected with the "gift" of lycanthropy but give in to their baser urges.

Eva the Provider

Saint of Agriculture.

Maxim: Lady Spring.
Symbol: A Sheaf of Wheat
Description: Eva is seen as a benevolent god who likes to see her followers safe and comfortable. When a farmer has a better than average crop, they praise Eva, but when hail or locusts ruin them, they blame Mar or Agrinor.

Familor the Chained

Saint of Keeping Secrets and Pacts.

Maxim: The pact maker.
Symbol: Black Manacles
Description: Familor likes to strike bargains. He will offer salvation, dangling it in front of his victim in exchange for a favor, until that favor is complete Familor keeps the bargainers’ soul. Those who have successfully fulfilled one of his pacts tell of horrific dreams for months and years after; it is rumored that Familor likes to torture his souls while they are in his possession. Some Warlocks look to Familor for power, swearing fealty to him in exchange for servitude.

Known Sects/Cults: The Fulcrum - An elite assassins guild. A contract with the fulcrum, once paid for and signed, is followed to the letter as zealously as if Familor himself had signed it.

Great Grandmother Stemrot

Powerful Night Hag

Maxim:
Symbol: A Bleeding Inverted Rose
Common Followers: Some Hags and Warlocks
Description: Little is known about Great Grandmother Stemrot other than her name and that other Hags fear her. Her location is unknown, but her hand has been seen in some of the crueler plots that have affected the Prime Material Plane.

Ishtar the Beautiful

Saint of Ambition, Luck, and Fate

Maxim: The nudge.
Symbol: Two white dice - one pip and six pips
Description: The Lady Ishtar had a larger following amongst the denizens of underworld until losing much of her power during the First Battle of Heaves Fall; this allowed Banath to usurp much of Ishtar’s domain. Ishtar does not discriminate. All intelligent creatures want something, and all will risk things to get it: Ishtar is merely there to see, or help, the dice fall. Her name will often be used as a curse when befallen by a bad decision, or ill luck.

Kelmarik the Confessor

Saint of Vengeance and Revenge, Seeking Justice.

Maxim: The Slow Burn.
Symbol: An Inverted Flame.
Description:Sister to Dulmarth. Kelmarik is invoked by those seeking vengeance, payback, or revenge. Rarely steadily worshiped (except by bounty hunters), she is turned to in times of need with great zeal. Her name is often spoken as a curse.

Kirallin

Goddess of Light and Life.

Maxim: the Lady of Light.
Symbol: A Lily
Description: Kirallin is a benevolent goddess worshipped by many who find family and home most important in life. She is often portrayed as a kind elderly grandmother.

Kulindra (Kul)

Goddess of Death

Maxim: She who waits. The caster of nets.
Symbol: A Silver Net
Description: When a mortal dies its soul is ushered to Kulindra’s Gate, there it sits in judgement from Kulindra herself for how the soul lived in life. Priests of Kulindra will seek out, and attempt to destroy, necromancy at any costs as it goes against the natural order of the universe. The clergy of Kulindra do not seem to age, you may see an old priestess, but she will look in her early twenties. Most priests and priestesses of the goddess of death have a morose aura about them, and lifeless eyes. Kulindra is at odds with The Raven Queen as she is the Goddess of Undeath.

Loial the Tireless

Saint of Travel and Adventure

Maxim: The Tireless Wanderer.
Symbol: A Worn Boot or A Wagon Wheel.
Description: Loial is also known as the god of the open road. Worshiped by merchants and wanderers alike, many will say a quick prayer to Loial before starting a long journey.

Mar the Immortal Captian

Saint of Sails

Maxim: The Immortal Captain
Symbol: Mar's Fishhook
Description: Back when they were mortals, Mar was Tarpalm's captain on the Dreadnought's Regard. Tarpalm led a mutiny on the ship and threw Mar overboard into the deep. He waited there, biding his time while Tarpalm usurped his domain. A chance drowning by another sailor many years later allowed him an escape and a chance to reclaim his domain.

Agents:

Jeremy Gifford:Another victim of a sea voyage gone awry, he sank, by chance, in the same vicinity of Mar. A shell of his former self, Mar recruited Giff into his service - granting him powers in exchange for helping return the god to his former glory.

Neltelantol

The Great Dryad

Maxim: The Mother of Trees
Symbol: A Gold Leaf
Common Followers: Most Elves in the Feywild, and the Majority of Elves in the Prime Material Plane.
Description: Neltelantol dwells deep in the Feywild. She is the source of most of the Elven Spellweavers powers, making the majority of Tel’Na’Gren and Tarcian spell casters warlocks. She watches over her subjects in the Fey, and in the Prime Material Plane, with endearment and love as they share the same affinity for growing and green things as she does.

Relomdra the Demon Saint

Saint of Havoc and Demon Worshipers

Maxim: The Sower of Seeds
Symbol: Blue Sword on a Blood Drop
Description: Former Goddess of War and Strife. Relomdra was cast into the Abyss by The Unbound One during the Second Battle of Heavens Fall, she quickly returned to the battlefield as a Demon Lord leading an army from The Abyss. She was struck down and was forced to retreat into the Abyss at the Temple of the Three Sisters.

Sarillen the Deceiver

Saint of Uncovering Lies and Deception.

Maxim: The Unseen.
Symbol: A Piece of Reflective Metal.
Description: Sa-Sarig gained power and influence after The First Battle of Heaves Fall when he took over the illusion domain from Sarzerik who was destroyed. Those who worship Sa-Sarig often embody his mischievous nature.

Somniatella the Starwalker

Saint of interpreting Dreams, Nightmares, Saint of the Night

Maxim: She who walks in shadow.
Symbol: Four Pointed Star
Description: Middle Sister to Dicium and Vora. Somniatella has been known to visit users dreams and give them advice or assistance; the sleeper will often wake up with an item they do not remember owning. She is known as Goddess of the Night, as such, some Vampires have taken up worship of her.

The Ordained: The most gifted of Somniatellas followers are known as Dream Walkers, able to physically enter peoples dreams and communicate with them or use it as a method of travel by exiting a user’s dream within their physical vicinity.

Sylinus

The Great Satyr

Maxim: The Horn of Battle
Symbol: Satyr Horns
Common Followers: Most Elves in the Feywild, and the Majority of Elves in the Prime Material Plane.
Description: While all elves acknowledge Neltelentol’s power and domain over nature, many elves also pay respect to Sylinus, the great satyr protector who guards the Fey from enemies embodies the very essence of an elven warrior: vigilance, dedication, and passion. Elven warriors who have proven themselves in battle are often seen with satyr horns embroidered on their armor.

Tiana

Goddess of Magic

Maxim: The Lady of Magic, The High Mage.
Symbol: A Red Sunburst
Description: The original Goddess of Magic dating back before The First Battle of Heaves Fall, she went into hiding and was working on restoring her power under the guise of Enchantress Tiana, Mage of the Rillnon Court. It was Tiana who instructed Amys to wait and assist the Stone Bearers when they arrived in the Temple of the Three Sisters. At the conclusion of The Second Battle of Heavens Fall, Tiana regained her full power.

Agents:

Giannis Fairburn:Giannis was one of the adventurers that catalyzed the Second Battle of Heavens Fall. Previous to those events, she gifted a ring to Giannis that saved his life. He now serves her for the greater good. Giannis is the original creator of the spell: Fairburn’s Parley.

Tonks

God of Knowledge

Maxim: The Chronicler.
Symbol: A Codex Scroll
Description: Tonks is a neutral god that parts his wisdom on those he sees fit. Students are known to say a prayer to Tonks before an important exam. There is mention of Tonks in the Rescue of the Belevar Kingdom, a children’s story about the hero Talon, who enters the Warren Arcana and seeks out Tonks to find the answer to an illness that has befallen his queen. This has never been corroborated as anything other than fantasy.

The Ordained: Those who rise high enough in the church of knowledge take on the moniker 'Chronicler'. They are dedicated to preserving the worlds information and are a font of knowledge for those that seek it.

Thystre the Unbound

God of Madness, Darkness, and Chaos.

Maxim: The Unbound One
Symbol: unknown
Description: The Unbound One is a being of pure madness and Chaos. As such, he is not bound by the rules of the other gods follow and often walks the Prime Material Plane causing havoc and devastation to further his own ends. He was once defeated and locked away in the Prime Material Plane by an alliance of the other gods, including The Abstainer. He was mistakenly set free by the Stone Bearers. He was last seen at the Battle at Three Sisters resembling a frail old man wielding an oversized scythe. He is responsible for the death of Astalon, and the turning of Relomdra into a Demon Lord.

Vora the Harbinger

Saint of Conquest

Maxim: The Lady of Swords.
Symbol: Two Thin Swords in Parallel
Description: Youngest sister of Dicium and Somniatella. Vora was the one who directed the elf Merrowquin and set into motion the events preceding The Second Battle of Heavens Fall. It was in the Temple of the Three Sisters where she was released from the stones and fought with Relomdra. Relomdra was defeated and Vora became the only effigy of War. Her followers now hunt down the remaining followers of Demon Lord Relomdra who have become tainted by the Abyss.

Agents:

Thule: One of the Stone Bearers, he is the Harbinger of Battle and serves at Vora’s whim. His last known whereabouts were in northern Rymura, hunting down Demon Lord Relomdra’s cultists.

Deceased

Astalon

Deceased God of Law, Order, and Justice

Maxim: The Peacemaker.
Symbol: A Gold Key
Common Followers: Barristers, kings, nobility, headsman, constables.
Description: The divine representative of peace, order, and law, Astalon was devoutly worshipped by nobility, magistrates, and constables. He was slain by The Unbound One during The Second Battle of Heavens Fall and his domain was inherited by Dicium.

Guis-wa

Deceased God of Endurance, Prowess, and Physical Skill.

Maxim: The Tireless Runner.
Symbol: An Arm - Shown Flexed.
Common Followers: Soldiers, duelists, festival attendees.
Description: The benevolent god of endurance and skill was primarily worshiped at festivals and sporting events. Soldiers and duelists were also known to worship Guis-wa, even referring to the network of trenches, wooden barricades, and earthen berms between Parnathen and Tarmentis as Guis-wa’s Wall. He was slain in the Temple of the Three Sisters during the Second Battle of Heavens Fall by Kelmarik, with help from her brother Dulmarth.

Tarmeer

Deceased God of Storms and of the Sea

Maxim: The Sudden Squall, The Fickle Mistress.
Symbol: A Lightning Bolt or Anchor.
Common Followers: Sailors, island dwellers, and fishermen.
Description: During the First Battle of Heaves Fall Tarpalm, god of storms, and Numeer, goddess of the sea, were defeated. Already lovers, the two chose to combine their powers and meld into one entity lest they be permanently destroyed, cast out, and forgotten. Tarmeer was seen as neither good nor evil yet was staunchly worshiped by sailors and fishermen. Tarmeer’s name was often used as a curse, or a boon, depending on the situation. Tarmeer fell at the hands of The Stone Bearers, with the help of Amys, at the Temple of the Three Sisters.
