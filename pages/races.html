Rymura Races and Subraces

The below are the customized races of Rymura...

Dwarves

Kingdoms rich in ancient grandeur, halls carved into the roots of mountains, the echoing of picks and hammers in deep mines and blazing forges, a commitment to clan and tradition, and a burning hatred of goblins and orcs – these common threads unite all dwarves.

  • Ability Score Increase. When determining your character's ability scores, increase one score by 2 and increase a different score by 1, or increase three different scores by 1.
  • Age. Dwarves, barring external factors such as wars and famine, can live between 400 - 500 years.
  • Size. Your size is Medium.
  • Speed. Your base walking speed is 25 feet.
  • Darkvision. You can see in dim light within 60 feet of you, dimming at 30 feet, as if it were bright light, and in darkness as if it were dim light. You can't discern color in darkness, only shades of gray.
  • Dwarven Resistance. You have advantage on saving throws against magic.
  • Languages. You can speak, read, and write Common, Dwarven, and one other language.

Subraces

Line of Drome

Drome was a warrior of great renown. Afte the First Exodus, he led his clan high into the Silver Wreath mountains, displacing the goblins and elves in the area. The clan made their home by carving directly into the rockface of the mountains and building wooden longhouses. Supplied by their highland farms, the Line of Drome clings fiercely to their traditions and has securely held their idyllic vale for over 2000 years.

Clan Drome is currently ruled by Berenia, the sixth daughter, who has blood ties to Drome himself.

  • Dwarven Toughness. Your hit point maximum increases by 1, and it increases by 1 every time you gain a level.
  • Armor Training. You have proficiency in all armors.

Line of Belgrieg

Belgrieg, known as the first builder, pioneered many advancements in dwarven engineering. His teachings on pulls, levers, and gears during the Age of Legends catapulted his clan into being the best miners in Rymura. Known to the other races as 'The Diggers', Belgriegians get along well with the other humanoid races in the area, even boasting cordial relation with the Tel'Na'Gren.

Diggers of Clan Belgrieg are renowned for their ability to find, and then mine, various materials both common and rare. Hiring a small team of Belgriegians is expensive, but on a Kingdom level it is a small price to pay for finding marble, silver or other precious deposits.

Led by their merchant council, Clan Belgrieg practices 'General Apprenticeships'. Dwarves from age 20-40 are expected to apprentice at a wide variety of crafts before, at the Age of Choosing, they select a profession that suits them. Because most Belgriegians have developed rudimentary skills in many crafts, communities quickly recovery from crises like fires or raids.

  • Dwarven Toughness. Your hit point maximum increases by 1, and it increases by 1 every time you gain a level.
  • Armor Training. You have proficiency in all armors.

Line of Gronbauch

Prior to the First War of Extermination, Strike Leader Gronbach felt, much like the elves, that they should invade and eliminate their neighbors before they become a problem. Unlike the unified elves, Gronbauch didn't have the backing of his leaders and was exiled from the Dwarven lands. Gronbach led many like-minded dwarves deep underground, eventually establishing a hold and clan. There, deep in their underground fortresses, the Line of Gronbauch plot their revenge against their cousins in the world above. So far, their plotted revenge has not materialized as they constantly struggle against the other denizens of the world below.

  • Dwarven Toughness. Your hit point maximum increases by 1, and it increases by 1 every time you gain a level.
  • Armor Training. You have proficiency in all armors.

Elves

Kingdoms rich in ancient grandeur, halls carved into the roots of mountains, the echoing of picks and hammers in deep mines and blazing forges, a commitment to clan and tradition, and a burning hatred of goblins and orcs – these common threads unite all dwarves.

  • Ability Score Increase. When determining your character's ability scores, increase one score by 2 and increase a different score by 1, or increase three different scores by 1.
  • Age. Although elves reach physical maturity at about the same age as humans, they are still considered children by other elves until their 100th year. Lorekeepers have been known to live between 1400 - 1500 year of age, but after their first century, they tend not to leave their ancestral homes.
  • Size. Your size is Medium.
  • Speed. Your base walking speed is 30 feet.
  • Darkvision. You can see in dim light within 60 feet of you, dimming at 30 feet, as if it were bright light, and in darkness as if it were dim light. You can't discern color in darkness, only shades of gray.
  • Graceful Nature. Naturally incling to a graceful gait and a light step, you have proficiency in the stealth skill.
  • Languages. You can speak, read, and write Common, Elven, and one other language.

  • Size. Halflings about 3 feet tall and weight around 40 pounds. Your size is small.
  • Speed. Your base walking speed is 25 feet.
  • Lucky. When you roll a 1 on an attack roll, ability check, or saving throw, you can reroll the die. Your must use the new result, even if it is a 1.

  • Nimble. You can move through the space of any creature that is a larger size then yours.
