Rymura Races - Humans

While Humans may lack the longevity of Elves, the nimbleness of the Hin, or the heartiness of Dwarves, they more than make up for it in two things: Ingenuity and reproduction.

"When the nomadic tribes of the Parna began to put down roots and take up agriculture, their population quickly outgrew that of our own. We were fresh off victories over the Gnomes and Hin and did not act swiftly enough. The small infection that should have been burned out has now spread across the face of the world." - Warleader Teshwynn, Tarcian Empire - 183 SHD.

Humans of Rymura

Due to their prodigious ability to increase their numbers, and the quick decision making (relative to the other races), and proclivity to make these decisions based on emotions; Humans were well situated to usurp the elves and become the dominant race after the Second Elven War of Extermination and subsequent victory over the Elven First Empire.

Since then, humans of Rymura have built a continent spanning empire, had that same empire crumble to dust, and have witnessed numerous kingdoms rise from the ashes.

All Humans share the following racial traits:

  • Age. Humans reach adulthood in their late teens and live less than a century.
  • Size. Your size is Medium.
  • Speed. Your base walking speed is 30 feet.
  • Detect the Supernatural. Undead, extraplanar creatures, and magic items in your vicinity makes you feel uneasy and your hair stands on end.
  • Ingenious. Gain one tool proficiency from Artisan Tools, Healing Kit, Herbalism Kit, Water or Land Vehicles, and one Armor or Weapon proficieny of your choice.
  • Languages. You can speak, read, and write Common and one other language.

Subraces

Standard Human

  • Ability Score Increase. Your ability scores each increase by 1.
  • Quick Learner. You gain proficiency in two skills of your choice.

Variant Human

  • Ability Score Increase. increase one score by 2 and increase two different scores by 1.
  • Feat. You gain one Feat of your choice.

Teeque

Offspring of a human and a halfling. The Teeque's inclined to a human lifestyle generally do not have problems being accepted into human towns and cities because their smaller stature and weight make them appear less threatening to the average citizen.

  • Ability Score Increase. When determining your character's ability scores, increase one score by 2 and increase a different score by 1, or increase three different scores by 1.
  • Age. While Teeque's mature at the same rate as full blooded humans, they tend to live longer lives with some exceeding two centuries.
  • Hin Heritage:
    • Naturally Nimble. You can take the Disengage or Hide action as a bonus action on each of your turns.
    • Languages. You also speak, read, and write Hin.
