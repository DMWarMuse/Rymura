Rymura Races - Elves

"Wary be those who pull the eye of the Empire, for when that beast awakens: the mountains shake." - Seargent Two-Winds, Last Stand of the Third Irregulars: Tarwins Gap - 653 SED.

Vigilance, Dedication, and Passion, these are the halmarks of the elven race. More reclusive than in the age of the First Empire, Tel'Na'Gren and Tarcian elves can still be found wandering the world of Man.

"That the elves are a fractured race is a surprise to no one. Once the undisputed rulers of the known world, now spectres and shades perferring to hide in the forests and jungles to the point where seeing two together in any human city has becomes an oddity." - Baron Delmont, Green Cap Historians

Elves of Rymura

All Elves share the following racial traits:

  • Age. Humans reach adulthood in their late teens and live less than a century.
  • Size. Your size is Medium.
  • Speed. Your base walking speed is 30 feet.
  • Detect the Supernatural. Undead, extraplanar creatures, and magic items in your vicinity makes you feel uneasy and your hair stands on end.
  • Ingenious. Gain one tool proficiency from Artisan Tools, Healing Kit, Herbalism Kit, Water or Land Vehicles, and one Armor or AWeapon proficieny of your choice.
  • Languages. You can speak, read, and write Common and one other language.

History

  • Gremorian Incursion

    A fleet on monsters land on the west coast of Rymura, heroes of Clan Tyndrus join the fight and repulse the invaders

    Age of Myth
  • Tides of War

    Clan Tyndrus build a large fleet and sail west towards the Gremorian Homeland

  • Time of the Mad Ones Rage

    Clan Aphos attacks their neighboring clans in the south east. The war lasts decades. Eventually Clan Aphos is pushed off the mainland and confined to the Isle of Sorrowful Tears, later known as the Aphos Island.

  • Last Contact of the Tyndrus

    A single longboat arrives on the western shores of Rymura, a dead Tyndrus inside clutching a battered tome, the tome tells of a great battle in the Gremorian homeland, against the Formorian. This marks the last time a Tyndrus has been seen.
