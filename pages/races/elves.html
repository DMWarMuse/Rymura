Rymura Races - Elves

"Wary be those who pull the eye of the Empire, for when that beast awakens: the mountains shake." - Seargent Two-Winds, Last Stand of the Third Irregulars: Tarwins Gap - 653 SED.

Vigilance, Dedication, and Passion, these are the halmarks of the elven race. More reclusive than in the age of the First Empire, Tel'Na'Gren and Tarcian elves can still be found wandering the world of Man.

"That the elves are a fractured race is a surprise to no one. Once the undisputed rulers of the known world, now spectres and shades perferring to hide in the forests and jungles to the point where seeing two together in any human city has becomes an oddity." - Baron Delmont, Green Cap Historians

Elves of Rymura

All Elves share the following racial traits:

  • Ability Score Increase. When determining your character's ability scores, increase one score by 2 and increase a different score by 1, or increase three different scores by 1.
  • Age. Dwarves, barring external factors such as wars and famine, can live between 400 - 500 years.
  • Size. Your size is Medium.
  • Speed. Your base walking speed is 25 feet.
  • Darkvision. You can see in dim light within 60 feet of you, dimming at 30 feet, as if it were bright light, and in darkness as if it were dim light. You can't discern color in darkness, only shades of gray.
  • Dwarven Resistance. You have advantage on saving throws against magic.
  • Languages. You can speak, read, and write Common, Dwarven, and one other language.

Subraces

Aphosian

Drome was a warrior of great renown. After the First Exodus, he led his clan high into the Silver Wreath mountains, displacing the goblins and elves in the area. The clan made their home by carving directly into the rockface of the mountains and building wooden longhouses. Supplied by their highland farms, the Line of Drome clings fiercely to their traditions and has securely held their idyllic vale for over 2000 years.

Clan Drome is currently ruled by Berenia, the sixth daughter, who has blood ties to Drome himself.

  • Dwarven Toughness. Your hit point maximum increases by 1, and it increases by 1 every time you gain a level.
  • Armor Training. You have proficiency in all armors.

Holmnaithe

Harthorn, known as the first builder, pioneered many advancements in dwarven engineering. His teachings during the Age of Legends catapulted his clan into being the best miners in Rymura. Known to the other races as 'The Diggers', Harthorngarians get along well with the other humanoid races in the area, even boasting cordial relation with the Tel'Na'Gren.

Diggers of Clan Harthorn are renowned for their ability to find, and then mine, various materials both common and rare. Hiring a small team of Harthorngarians is expensive, but on a Kingdom level it is a small price to pay for finding marble, silver or other precious deposits.

Led by their merchant council, Clan Harthorn practices 'General Apprenticeships'. Dwarves from age 20-40 are expected to apprentice at a wide variety of crafts before, at the Age of Choosing, they select a profession that suits them. Because most Harthorngarians have developed rudimentary skills in many crafts, communities quickly recovery from crises like fires or raids.

  • Artisan Expertise. You gain proficiency with two kinds of artisan's tools of your choice. Your proficiency bonus is doubled for any ability check you make that uses either of the chosen proficiencies.
  • Speed. Your base walking speed increases by 5 feet.

Setnael

Prior to the First War of Extermination, Strike Leader Gronbach felt, much like the elves, that they should invade and subjugate (or eliminate) their neighbors before they become a problem. Unlike the unified elves, Gronbauch didn't have the backing of his leaders and was exiled from the Dwarven lands. Gronbach led many like-minded dwarves deep underground, eventually establishing a hold and clan. There, deep in their underground fortresses, the Line of Gronbauch plot their revenge against their cousins in the world above. So far, their plotted revenge has not materialized as they constantly struggle against the other denizens of the world below.

  • Psionic Fortitude. You have advantage on saving throws you make to avoid or end the charmed, dazed, or stunned condition on yourself, you also have resistance to Psychic damage.
  • Superior Darkvision. You can see in dim light within 120 feet of you, dimming at 60 feet, as if it were bright light, and in darkness as if it were dim light. You can't discern color in darkness, only shades of gray..

  • Time of the Mad Ones Rage

    Clan Aphos attacks their neighboring clans in the south east. The war lasts decades. Eventually Clan Aphos is pushed off the mainland and confined to the Isle of Sorrowful Tears, later known as the Aphos Island.
